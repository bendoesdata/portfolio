Benjamin Cooley is a creative technologist focusing on visualization and human-computer interaction.

His work explores new ways of seeing and experiencing information through data visualization, creative coding, information design, and machine learning to create intuitive user interfaces.

Ben currently works as a Visualization Software Engineer in the Pattern lab at the Broad Institute. He is also an adjunct lecturer at Northeastern University's College of Arts, Media and Design (CAMD).

After completing a Bachelor's degree in journalism, Ben received an MSc in Computational Journalism from Goldsmiths, University of London. Though his focus is now on software tool-making, Ben continues to use his background in journalism to create narrative driven designs. Ben also enjoys using technology as a generative tool, and often uses procedural techniques for writing and performing music as b.dexter.
